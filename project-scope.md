# The Problem

Most finance apps force you to fill out a form for every transaction. Pick a category. Type the amount. Add the date. Add a description. Repeat five times a day — and within a week, you've given up. Receipts pile up in your wallet, unread.

The friction of using the tool is higher than the value you get from it.

# The Solution

A finance tracker that listens to you instead of demanding input.

You type a quick note — *"lunch at warteg A, spent 200rb"* — and the app figures out the rest. It knows that's an expense, the amount is 200,000 rupiah, the category is food, and today is the date. You just confirm.

Or snap a photo of a receipt, and the app reads the merchant, total, and date for you.

The app installs to your phone's home screen like a real app, opens with one tap, learns your spending categories over time, and caps its own AI spending so it stays cheap to run. Each user gets a private account — no one else can see your data.

# Features

## Your dashboard
- A quick-add bar to log a transaction in one line
- This month's summary at a glance: total income and total spending
- Two charts: income vs expense over time, and where your money is going by category
- Your most recent transactions, just below

## Logging transactions
- Type a sentence, confirm a structured transaction
- Snap a photo of a receipt, get the same result
- Browse your full history, filtered by month
- Edit or delete any transaction, anytime

## Categories
- Starts with 8 sensible defaults: Food, Transport, Salary, and more
- The AI invents new ones when a transaction doesn't fit an existing category
- Your list grows naturally as you use the app

## Safety and polish
- A daily cap on AI spending per user, with a warning when you approach it
- Installs to your phone's home screen and opens fullscreen, like a native app
- Indonesian Rupiah with shorthand parsing — "200rb" means 200,000, "2jt" means 2,000,000
- Your data stays private to your account
